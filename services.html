Services

Veterinary Services

The veterinary clinic provides essential services catering to various needs, including spaying and neutering, vaccinations, and microchipping for cats, dogs, non-profit rescue groups, and feral cats. These services are crucial for maintaining the health and well-being of pets, ensuring they lead happy and safe lives. Whether it's preventive care through vaccinations or enhancing identification with microchips, our clinic is dedicated to supporting both individual pet owners and community welfare initiatives.

Grooming

Our grooming services cater to both cats and dogs, offering a range of essential treatments to keep your pets looking and feeling their best. We provide baths to maintain cleanliness, professional haircuts tailored to breed standards or owner preference, precise nail trims to ensure comfort and health, thorough ear cleanings for hygiene, and combo outs for comprehensive grooming sessions. Whether your pet needs a routine grooming session or specific treatments, our expert groomers are committed to providing gentle care and top-notch service to enhance your pet's well-being and appearance.

Trap Neuter Release

TNR, or trap-neuter-return, is the humane method recommended for managing and reducing feral and free-roaming cat populations. Feral cats, born and raised in the wild or abandoned, live independently and typically avoid human contact. Managed colonies where these cats are spayed, neutered, vaccinated, and provided with food, water, and shelter allow them to live contentedly. However, outdoor life poses risks. NAWS Low Cost Spay & Neuter Clinic offers essential services for free-roaming cats, including spaying, neutering, vaccinations, and ear tipping for identification. You can support community cats by learning about TNR and using humane traps to bring them to the clinic for necessary care.
